Vietnamese pho

Introduction

Pho is a Vietnamese soup consisting of broth, rice noodles, herbs, and meat. It is a popular street food in Vietnam and the specialty of a number of restaurant chains around the world. Pho is primarily served with either beef or chicken. The Hanoi and Saigon styles of pho differ by noodle width, sweetness of broth, and choice of herbs. The origin of pho and its name is a subject of scholarly debate. The traditional home of pho is reputed to be the villages of Van Cu and Dao Cung in the Nam Dinh Province. According to villagers, pho was eaten in Van Cu long before the French colonial period when it was popularized. The origins of pho are relatively unrecorded, and there is little agreement over the cultural influences that led to its development in Vietnam, although it is likely that the dish was brought over

Ingredients

  • 4-5 lbs beef bones
  • large onion, halved and unpeeled
  • Thinly sliced raw meat
  • whole garlic
  • Fresh ginger
  • Whole spices: star anise, cloves, cinnamon sticks, black cardamom pods, fennel seeds, coriander seeds
  • salt
  • Fish sauce
  • Sugar

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 450 Farenheit
  2. Place the beef bones and onion on a baking sheet and roast for 30 minutes
  3. Place the roasted bones and onion in a large stockpot and cover with cold water
  4. Bring to a boil and then reduce to a simmer
  5. Skim off any foam that rises to the top
  6. Add the garlic, ginger, and spices to the pot
  7. Simmer for 3-4 hours
  8. Strain the broth and return to the pot
  9. Add the salt, fish sauce, and sugar to the broth
  10. Bring to a boil and then reduce to a simmer
  11. Prepare the rice noodles according to the package instructions
  12. Place the noodles in a bowl and top with the thinly sliced raw meat
  13. Ladle the hot broth over the noodles and meat
  14. Serve with fresh herbs, bean sprouts, lime wedges, and chili slices
